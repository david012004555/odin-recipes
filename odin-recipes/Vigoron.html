Vigorón

Description:

A tropical harmony of Yucca, Chicharrén, and Slaw.

Ingredients:

The Base:

  • 2 lbs Fresh yucca (cassava), peeled and cut into large chunks.

  • 1 tsp Salt.

  • Water (enough to cover the yucca).

The Crunch:

  • 1/2 lb High-quality Chicharrón (pork rinds). Look for the "meatier" kind that has both the crispy skin and a bit of fried pork meat attached.

The "Vigorón" Slaw (Curtido):

  • 1/2 Head of green cabbage, shredded very thinly.

  • 2 Medium tomatoes, diced.

  • 1/2 White onion, finely chopped.

  • 1/2 cup Bitter orange juice (naranja agria).

  • 1 tbsp Vinegar (optional, for extra tang).

  • Salt to taste.

Optional: Boiled mimbro (bilimbi fruit) or sliced spicy chilies (chile congo) for an authentic kick.

Instructions:

Prepare the Yucca (The "Reventada" Style):

  • Place the yucca chunks in a large pot with enough water to cover them by an inch. Add salt.

  • Bring to a boil, then reduce to a simmer.
  • Pro-Tip: Once the yucca is nearly soft, add a cup of cold water to the pot. This "shock" helps the yucca fibers open up and achieve that classic "reventada" (burst) texture.
  • Drain once it’s tender enough to be pierced easily with a fork. Remove the woody core from the center of the chunks.

Make the Fresh Slaw:

  • In a large bowl, mix the shredded cabbage, tomatoes, and onion.
  • Pour the bitter orange juice over the mixture and season with salt.

Pro-Tip:Let the salad sit for at least 15 minutes before serving. This allows the cabbage to soften slightly and soak up the acidity.

Assemble the Dish:

  • Place a generous portion of steaming hot yucca on the leaf (or plate).

  • Add several large pieces of crispy chicharrón on top.

  • Crown the dish with a mountain of the cabbage slaw, ensuring some of the juice runs down to season the yucca.
