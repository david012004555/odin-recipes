Gallo Pinto

Description:

The secret to a perfect Gallo Pinto lies in two things: day-old rice (which is drier and fries better) and small red beans.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups Long-grain white rice (cooked the day before and refrigerated).
  • 1.5 cups Red silk beans (frijoles rojos de seda), cooked and slightly drained.
  • ½ cup Bean broth (the liquid from the pot).
  • ½ Large white onion, finely chopped.
  • ½ Green bell pepper (chiltoma), finely chopped.
  • 3 tbsp Vegetable oil (don't be shy; the oil carries the flavor).
  • Salt to taste.

Instructions

  1. Sauté the Aromatics : Heat the oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add the onion and bell pepper. Sauté until the onion is translucent and slightly golden. This creates the flavor base (el sofrito).
  2. Fry the Beans: Add the beans to the skillet along with the ½ cup of broth. Let them simmer and fry for about 3–5 minutes. You want the broth to reduce slightly and the beans to begin absorbing the flavor of the oil and onions.
  3. Incorporate the Rice: Add the cold rice to the skillet. Use a wooden spoon to break up any clumps. Stir thoroughly so that every grain of rice is coated in the red bean liquid.
  4. The "Toasting" Phase: Lower the heat to medium and keep stirring occasionally for about 8–10 minutes. The goal is for the moisture to evaporate until the rice is "painted" (pinto) deep red and the texture becomes slightly crispy on the edges.
  5. Seasoning: Taste and add salt as needed. If you like a deeper umami flavor, this is when you add a dash of Lizano or Worcestershire sauce.

Tip:If you want an extra layer of flavor, you can add a splash of Salsa Lizano or Worcestershire sauce(known as Salsa Inglesa in Nicaragua) during the frying process.
